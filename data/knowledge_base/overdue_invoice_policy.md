# Overdue Invoice Escalation Policy

Category: billing

## Definition of Overdue

An invoice is considered overdue when payment has not been processed within the agreed payment terms:
- Net 15: Overdue after 15 calendar days from receipt
- Net 30: Overdue after 30 calendar days from receipt
- Net 45: Overdue after 45 calendar days from receipt

## Escalation Timeline

When a vendor reports an overdue invoice, the following escalation process applies:

### Stage 1: Initial Investigation (0-2 business days)
- Finance Team verifies invoice status in AP system
- Checks if three-way match is complete
- Confirms no holds or discrepancies exist
- Response sent to vendor within 2 business days

### Stage 2: Priority Processing (2-5 business days)
- If invoice is valid and matched, it is flagged for priority payment
- Payment scheduled for the next available payment run (Tuesday or Thursday)
- Vendor notified of expected payment date

### Stage 3: Management Escalation (5+ business days)
- If unresolved after 5 business days, escalated to AP Manager
- AP Manager reviews within 1 business day
- Resolution target: 7 business days from initial complaint

## Late Payment Interest

- Interest is NOT automatically applied to overdue invoices
- Vendors may request interest per their contract terms
- Standard late payment rate: 1.5% per month on the overdue amount
- Interest claims must be submitted via the portal with supporting documentation

## Dispute Resolution

If the vendor disputes the payment status:
1. Submit a formal dispute via VQMS Portal or email
2. Include: Invoice number, PO number, amount, and reason for dispute
3. Finance Team investigates within 3 business days
4. Resolution options: immediate payment, partial payment, or payment plan
